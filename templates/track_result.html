How to Identify Phishing Attempts

Here are key indicators to help you spot phishing emails:

Check the Sender's Address

Phishing emails often come from addresses that look similar to legitimate ones but have slight variations or misspellings.

Verify Links Before Clicking

Hover over links to see the actual URL. If it looks suspicious or doesn't match the claimed destination, don't click it.

Be Cautious With Attachments

Avoid opening attachments from unknown or unexpected sources as they may contain malware.

Look for Urgency or Threats

Phishing emails often create a false sense of urgency or use threatening language to prompt immediate action.

Check for Spelling and Grammar

Many phishing emails contain noticeable spelling mistakes and grammatical errors.

Verify Unusual Requests

If an email requests sensitive information or unusual actions, verify through another communication channel.
